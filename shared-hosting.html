FAQ's What is Shared Hosting

What is shared hosting ?

Shared hosting is a type of web hosting where a single physical server hosts multiple sites. Because your website shares space with other websites, the resources such as disk space and bandwidth (people who visit your website) could be limited because those resources are being shared, however, Shared hosting is a great solution for those who are looking for a low cost solution and aren't expecting high volumes of traffic.

The difference between shared and vps hosting

Users of Shared hosting all share the resources on the server their websites are on whereas VPS all the resources belong to you. There is no guarantee of RAM or CPU with Shared hosting. Shared hosting also does not provide full root access to the server, as opposed to VPS where users are given full root access to perform commands and make configuration changes to their hosting environment.

Who is shared webhosting suitable for

Share web hosting is most suitable for freelancers and entrepreneurs or those who are starting a business or have one newly establised, who's website doesn't get alot of traffic or they don't anticipate alot of website traffic. Because hosting resources are shared among others it can be limiting on traffic but still sufficent.
